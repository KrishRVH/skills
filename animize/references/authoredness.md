# Authoredness

Use this reference during Rewrite and Write jobs. Its purpose is to make prose
feel intentionally composed, not to add cosmetic irregularity or claim proof of
human authorship.

A reader experiences authorship through the choices behind the text: what is
selected, what is left out, what is made concrete, where judgment appears, how
uncertainty is handled, and how the piece moves. Grammar and sentence variety
alone are weak substitutes.

## 1. Selection and hierarchy

A deliberate piece does not give every available point equal weight. It chooses
a governing point, spends space according to consequence, and omits details
that do not help the reader.

Check:

- Can the main point be stated in one sentence?
- Does the longest section deserve to be longest?
- Is any paragraph present only because the source mentioned the topic?
- Are background and caveats placed where they affect interpretation?
- Does the piece end after its purpose is complete?

Repair by consolidating, reordering, or cutting. Do not preserve exhaustive
restatement merely because it looks thorough. Unique substantive source content
remains protected unless the caller authorizes compression.

## 2. Situated specificity

Natural prose is specific where specificity changes understanding. It names the
actor, action, mechanism, example, number, condition, or consequence supplied
by the source. It remains general where precision would be false or irrelevant.

Use the **anywhere test**: could an important sentence appear unchanged in an
unrelated article, report, or post? If so, replace it with supported particulars,
narrow it to the real claim, or remove it.

Do not fabricate specificity. A precise invention is worse than an honest
abstraction.

## 3. A visible but proportionate stance

A reader should be able to tell what the piece concludes, recommends, doubts,
or considers important. That does not require first person or personality.

- Put owned judgments close to their reasons.
- Preserve a meaningful `may`, `likely`, `usually`, `must`, or `will`.
- Remove hedge stacks that make uncertainty uninterpretable.
- Avoid symmetrical neutrality when the source supports a conclusion.
- Represent genuine counterevidence without turning every position into a
  balanced pair.

Do not manufacture conviction. Stronger tone is a substantive change when it
changes the claim.

## 4. Information movement

Each sentence should change the reader's state: add a fact, explain a mechanism,
qualify a claim, contrast alternatives, supply an example, draw a consequence,
or move to the next necessary question.

Watch for sentences that only:

- announce what the next sentence will say
- restate the preceding sentence in broader language
- summarize a paragraph that already ended clearly
- label a fact as important without naming the consequence
- bridge sections with a generic transition rather than a logical relation

A competent reader does not need every connection narrated. Leave a connection
implicit when adjacency and syntax make it clear.

## 5. Real constraints and friction

Human reasoning usually meets limits: an exception, trade-off, missing fact,
competing objective, operational constraint, or consequence. Include those
when the source contains them and when they affect the conclusion.

Do not add disclaimers merely to look candid. Do not invent ambivalence,
mistakes, or unresolved emotion. The useful form of texture is truthful
constraint.

## 6. Rhythm that follows thought

Sentence shape should reflect the relationship among ideas.

- A short sentence can state a consequence or decision.
- A longer sentence can hold a sequence, condition, or qualification together.
- Parallel syntax can clarify a genuine comparison.
- Repetition can establish a motif, term, or procedure.
- A fragment can work in a genre that permits it, but it is never required.

Repair cadence only when the repeated form becomes audible or obscures the
argument. Do not alternate lengths, punctuation, or openings by formula.

Natural lexical repetition is often better than synonym cycling. When one
thing remains one thing, keep calling it the same thing.

## 7. Paragraph shape

A paragraph should perform one local job, but it need not be a miniature essay.
Some paragraphs establish a claim; others provide evidence, explain a
mechanism, mark a limit, or carry a transition.

Allow:

- unequal paragraph lengths
- a paragraph that ends on evidence rather than a takeaway
- a qualification to remain in the same paragraph as the claim
- a brief paragraph when the shift genuinely deserves isolation
- a long paragraph when splitting it would sever one line of reasoning

Avoid repeated claim-explain-summary units and a heading over every small idea.

## 8. Openings

Open with the first thing the genre and reader need.

- **Report or memo:** finding, decision, request, or risk
- **Technical documentation:** task, outcome, prerequisites, or failure mode
- **Academic or scientific prose:** question, gap, method, or result in the
  field's expected form
- **Essay or newsletter:** tension, judgment, scene, question, or concrete fact
- **Professional post:** useful claim or observation, not engagement bait
- **Marketing:** audience problem, verifiable outcome, or differentiating
  mechanism

Cut runway such as declarations that the topic matters, promises to explore it,
or broad statements that could introduce any piece.

## 9. Endings

End with the final job of the piece:

- decision or recommendation
- implication
- action
- consequence
- synthesis that changes the reader's understanding
- an honest unresolved question

Do not append a moral, slogan, upbeat flourish, or complete recap merely to
create closure. An ending can be plain.

## 10. Genre-native defaults

### Analytical and technical

Favor explicit mechanisms, stable terminology, edge cases, and calibrated
claims. Avoid tutorial patter, marketing adjectives, and fake simplicity.

### Academic and scientific

Preserve field terminology, expected structure, citation conventions, passive
voice where conventional, and qualifications that affect validity. Avoid
injected personality and oversimplification.

### Essay, blog, and newsletter

Favor a visible point of view, concrete examples supplied by the source,
selective context, and flexible paragraph movement. Avoid distant generalities,
lesson-shaped endings, and borrowed intimacy.

### Professional communication

Lead with the request, decision, problem, or update. Name owners, dates, and
next actions when supplied. Avoid ceremony and excessive scene-setting.

### Marketing and product

Use verifiable outcomes, mechanisms, comparisons, and limitations. Replace
praise with evidence. Do not invent customer experience or social proof.

### Legal and policy

Preserve defined terms, scope, modality, exceptions, citations, and deliberate
repetition. Naturalness never outranks legal precision.

### Fiction and creative prose

Follow the brief's narrator, point of view, world facts, and stylistic
constraints. Invention is permitted only within the creative brief.

## 11. Voice modes

### Rebuild — default

Do not preserve source cadence, sentence shapes, transitions, headings, or
lexical habits. Use a direct, unshowy register suited to the genre. Preserve
only content, deliberate terminology, and owned evaluative force.

### Preserve — explicit request only

Record evidence-backed traits such as directness, density, humor, stance,
syntax, paragraph movement, punctuation, and roughness. Repair material defects
without replacing those traits.

### Match samples — explicit or sample-led request

Infer durable traits from more than one passage when possible. Separate writer
traits from topic vocabulary and genre requirements. Match functional
properties rather than memorable phrasing.

## 12. Continuous read tests

Use these after drafting:

- **Anywhere:** Which sentences could be pasted into an unrelated piece?
- **Sequence:** Does each sentence make the next one necessary rather than
  merely predictable?
- **Selection:** Does the space allocation reveal what matters?
- **Stance:** Can the reader identify the warranted conclusion or uncertainty?
- **Friction:** Are relevant limits and trade-offs present where they matter?
- **Necessity:** What can be removed without loss?
- **Template:** Do several paragraphs move in the same prebuilt pattern?
- **Ending:** Did the piece finish its job, or merely announce that it is done?

Repair only supported defects. Do not add quirks as evidence of authorship.
